How to choose a good racket for table tennis?

Table tennis is a game for adults and children, which has won many fans around the world. Even if you have never had to pick up a racket and play a game of ping-pong, you have probably repeatedly become a spectator of gambling sports matches in the yard, on summer playgrounds of holiday homes, in parks and even in the offices of serious organizations. Simple rules, the availability of sports equipment, the absence of the need for serious sports training have made table tennis a universal form of physical activity. Children like the game, it helps adults distract themselves from everyday worries, and promotes socialization of representatives of the older generation.

Ping-pong is played at the table, which is divided into two zones by a low net fixed with special fasteners. You will also need balls and, of course, a racket to play. We will tell you how to choose the right racket for table tennis in this article.

The main types of rackets for table tennis

Before buying a racket for table tennis, we suggest clarifying the important criteria for choosing sports equipment for ping-pong. The following main types of table tennis rackets are available for sale:

  • Ready-made, simple non-disassemble designs, will be an excellent choice for amateurs and novice athletes who are taking the first steps in table tennis, honing basic techniques. Prices for such rackets start from 30-50 hryvnias, but there are also more expensive options on sale. Pricing in this segment can be influenced by the number of stars (we will talk about them a little later), the quality of materials, and the popularity of the brand under which sports equipment is produced.
  • Composite rackets are premium segment rackets, which are mainly used by professionals and amateurs with a high level of training. The structure is assembled from several parts - the base, rubbers. Items can be purchased separately and exchanged to ensure optimal game performance.

Semi-professional rackets, in the production of which modern surface treatment technologies and high-quality materials are used. Such equipment is suitable for those who have mastered the technique perfectly. Among the four- and five-star models, there are both ready-made and prefabricated ones.

In the large "family" of attributes for the game of ping-pong, professional composite rackets stand out, in the design of which even the smallest nuances are not neglected. But once again I would like to emphasize that such rackets will not make a beginner a ping-pong ace. Mastering technique is easier and cheaper with an amateur racket.

Pads, sponges, bases of rackets for table tennis: classification

When choosing a racket for ping-pong, it may seem that all the models presented in the sporting goods store are "for one person". And, accordingly, the question arises: why such a divergence in prices? Several main components can be identified in the design of the racket, and the type of material from which they are made can affect the cost of the inventory.

Basis

About 85% of the total volume of the racket is the base, for the manufacture of which natural wood is used. Sometimes, as an alternative to wood, different types of plastic are used.

Reinforcing inserts — carbon, fiberglass, cellulose — make it possible to improve the consumer qualities of the product. The thickness of such a layer usually does not exceed 0.35 mm (up to 7.5% of the total thickness of the coating).

The construction of professional rackets often includes titanium plates, 3-5 layers of wood of different species (in some models - up to 13 layers!), which are fixed with special glue. When connecting the elements of the prefabricated structure, it is important to apply glue as evenly as possible, distributing it over the surface in a very thin layer.

Rubbers

How to choose pads for table tennis rackets? Why do most athletes focus their attention on this component of the structure? The pads cover the base from two sides, ensuring the optimal level of adhesion of the light ball to the surface. And if in the middle of the 20th century, the choice of materials for the manufacture of pads, options for the relief of their surface was very limited, then today manufacturers of sports equipment spend a lot of time and money on the development and testing of rackets with various pads.

Types of rackets by type of pads:

  • Identical - both surfaces are smooth, so there is no need to turn the racket during the game. The characteristics of such equipment may differ, and the provision of different speeds of the torque depends on this.
  • Different types of construction include smooth and studded pads, while one side provides strong rotation, the other - high feed speed. To change the tactics of the game, it is enough to turn the racket to the desired side. On sale you can find models for left-handed and right-handed people.
  • Attackers - are equipped with a smooth pad and an antispin (pad with elongated spikes) on the other. By flipping the pad, you can make your opponent seriously nervous, make them adapt to your chosen game tactics.

Sponge

A special spongy layer is placed under the pads, which allows for sufficient torque. The thickness of the sponge, depending on the type of racket, can be from 1 to 2.5 mm.

A thin spongy layer (1-1.7 mm) allows you to control the rotation of the ball at low speeds. Sponges with a thickness of 2 mm or more are ideal for implementing the attack technique. Rackets with intermediate characteristics are a universal solution. Do not forget to consider this parameter when choosing a tennis racket for table tennis!

The shape of the handle

Manufacturers of sports equipment offer amateurs and professionals ping-pong rackets with handles of the following shape:

  • Straight - ensure the convenience of turning the racket over.
  • Conical - represent an intermediate option between models with a straight handle and a dovetail holder.
  • Flared - not very convenient for turning, changing the grip during the game, but they also have their advantages.
  • Concave - an option for experienced players, they provide high speed of turns, convenience of changing the grip.
  • Anatomical — for long-term play, for beginners; hands get less tired during the game, the inventory feels like an "extension of the brush".
